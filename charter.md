## Preamble: Ratification and Authority

This document establishes the foundational operating system for the Regenerative
Media Alliance (RMA) and its affiliated projects.

By ratifying this Charter, all founders and members formally agree: **the power to
govern and run the organization is ceded from any individual founder to the
democratic rules, processes, and principles explicitly defined within this
repository.** No implicit expectations or dictates issued outside the authority of
this written governance structure shall hold power over the organization's members.
Any amendment to this Constitution must follow the democratic Pull Request process.

## 1. Organizational Purpose (The Anchor Circle)

Our overarching purpose is to build and sustain a collective-intelligence ecosystem
dedicated to shared endeavors that create measurable, positive impact.

**Core Mission:** To fuse diverse projects and organizational efforts into a
cohesive network capable of generating, distributing, and scaling valuable content
that drives positive change — to improve and amplify regenerative narratives, expand
the number of stories, increase the number and diversity of voices, and increase the
reach and influence of the work. *("The ESPN of regeneration.")*

**Current Phase Focus:** define and execute a singular, high-impact shared
narrative — **The Food Commons / "Heat in the Streets"** series — as the initial
vehicle for testing and proving the Alliance's combined content-creation,
distribution, and resource-sharing capabilities.

## 2. Core Values and Operating Principles

- **Consent:** Policy decisions advance as long as there are no reasoned objections
  demonstrating the policy would cause harm to the organization's purpose.
- **Equivalence:** All members, regardless of project or role, function as peers in
  the pursuit of collective goals.
- **Transparency:** All members hold a Duty of Transparency — sharing relevant
  metrics, project updates, and governance records needed for good decisions.
- **Effectiveness:** Continuous self-improvement through measurement, iterative
  evaluation, and adaptation.
- **Stewardship over Ownership:** No single individual or entity "owns" the
  collective. Members hold stewarding *rights* to utilize resources and stewarding
  *responsibilities* to protect them for future generations and non-human
  stakeholders. *"Cooperatively" owned is the key point — not a single person or a
  limited set* (Ed).

## 3. Organizational Structure: The Holarchy

A "holarchy" — self-regulating, interconnected units focused on shared outcomes.

- **The Alliance (Anchor Circle):** the outermost boundary holding the unified
  mission and all primary domains.
- **Sub-Circles (Projects / Focus Areas):** semi-autonomous groups (Media
  Production, Distribution, Curriculum, Research) with a clear purpose and explicit
  authority to self-organize internally.
- **Roles:** work is divided into Roles with clear purposes and accountabilities,
  not static job descriptions. Leadership (Point + Triad) and term limits are defined
  in [`docs/entity-and-structure.md`](docs/entity-and-structure.md); a fuller
  `roles-and-terms.md` is forthcoming (Release 1).

The cross-organizational methodology (RMA · regenBEE · Smoking Tigers) is documented
as a model in [`docs/methodology-three-roles.md`](docs/methodology-three-roles.md) —
framed as **delegation and coordination among peers, not an ownership hierarchy.**

## 4. Distributed Authority and Initiative

As a member filling a Role, you possess the ultimate authority to take any action
necessary to enact your Role's purpose, provided it does not violate this Charter or
an established policy.

- **Individual Initiative:** in situations requiring immediate action to prevent
  harm, members may take initiative outside standard process — then communicate it to
  all impacted roles and restore any tensions created.

## 6. Governance and Amendments

This organization operates as a living, collaborative document.

- **Governance Process:** any member may propose an amendment to this Charter or any
  policy/Role by submitting a Pull Request. Proposals are reviewed and integrated
  using the **Integrative Decision-Making Process**, relying on consent.
- **Phased Approach:** complex projects define a clear "light version" each phase
  with an explicit feedback loop into the next iteration.

## 7. Initial Operational Directives (Phase One)

- **Narrative Focus:** develop, test, and scale the Food Commons / Heat in the
  Streets narrative as a demonstration of combined capabilities.
- **Shared Resource Integration:** leverage shared resources (Q1 ecosystem,
  Riverside capture, collective knowledge) where appropriate.
- **IP Ownership:** content created by the Alliance is jointly owned by the
  organization, with IP rights secured through the operating agreement.
- **Legal Wrapper:** `[OPEN]` — a cooperative legal entity to protect assets and
  members (Q1 = California LLC, collectively owned, is one model to evaluate).

## Part II — Principles & Economic Model (from charter v0.03)

*Retained as the richer principle/economic layer. Token systems below are
**projects of the Alliance under active development, not binding rules** in v1.0.*

**Core Principles:** Stewardship over Ownership · Agency of the Whole (the collective
keeps adaptive agency separate from any individual, to protect from bad actors) ·
Ergodic & Resilient by Design (values all eight forms of capital, not financial
wealth alone) · Integrity & Diversity (ethical reporting; safe spaces for
neurodivergent + BIPOC creators) · Quid Pro Quo Contribution (skin in the game).

**Economic Model:** `[OPEN]` — an `economic-model.md` is forthcoming (Release 1):
structural profit-pooling (5–10% back to the commons), REV tokens (atomic jobs,
$1 REV = $1 at break-even), Hive Honey / Stewardship Credits (reciprocity), and
decoupling currency from governance. See the [glossary](docs/glossary.md) for the
token definitions.

**Media Operations:** Six Pockets of Life distribution (faith, service, learning,
society, work, leisure); non-extractive revenue (ad revenue + sponsorship cycled to
the commons); action-oriented anchor projects.

# Glossary of the Commons

The shared-language layer for RMA governance. Per [standards](../standards.md), terms
shared across files are defined here once and linked to. `[OPEN]` — trimmed to
load-bearing terms; expand via PR.

## Regenerative Media Alliance (RMA)
An independent, cooperatively owned media support system that amplifies narratives
catalyzing a regenerative future. Aligned with — not owned by — Quorum 1.

## Anchor Circle
The outermost holarchy boundary that holds RMA's unified mission and all domains.

## Holarchy
A structure of self-regulating, interconnected units (sub-circles) that are each both
wholes and parts of the larger alliance.

## Point
The single member who coordinates a circle or the alliance and holds key decisions — a
leader, **not** an executive (no unilateral executive authority).

## Triad
Three members who, with the Point, share leadership of a circle/entity and may hold
executive authority — the balance against any one person holding excessive power.

## Consent (Integrative Decision-Making)
A decision advances as long as there is no reasoned objection that it would cause harm
to RMA's purpose. The basis of RMA's governance and change process.

## Stewardship over Ownership
Members hold *stewarding rights* to use collective resources and *stewarding
responsibilities* to protect them. No individual owns the collective.

## Q-Git
The git-native governance change-flow (adopted from Quorum 1): changes via Release
branches + pull requests, approved by consent, recorded as a permanent public audit
trail. See [`../flows/readme.md`](../flows/readme.md).

## REV Points / Hive Honey  `[OPEN]`
Contribution-accounting tokens (REV = atomic-job value, $1=$1 at break-even; Hive
Honey = reciprocity/stewardship credit). Documented as a *project under development*,
not a binding rule in Release 0.

## The Three Roles  `[OPEN]`
RMA · regenBEE · Smoking Tigers — peer bodies coordinating the regenerative-media
movement. See [methodology-three-roles](methodology-three-roles.md).

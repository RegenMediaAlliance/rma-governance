# RMA Entity & Structure

How the Regenerative Media Alliance is organized, and how it relates to Quorum 1.

## What RMA is

RMA is an **independent, cooperatively owned media alliance** — its own entity, its
own governance (this repo), its own membership. It is not owned or governed by any
outside organization. *"Cooperatively owned is the key point — not a single person,
or a limited set"* (Ed). No single individual can own the collective.

## Relationship to Quorum 1 — alignment, not subordination

RMA partners with Quorum 1 and **chooses** to align with Q1's governance pattern:

- **Adopts Q1's Q-Git change-flow** as its own change process (see [`../flows/readme.md`](../flows/readme.md)) — proven, transparent, git-native.
- **Mirrors Q1's repo shape** (`docs/` = governance design, `flows/` = meta-governance) so the two are legible to shared members.
- **May use Q1's platform/tooling** (Notion, Riverside, shared infrastructure) by agreement.

What RMA does **not** do: cede final authority over its own governance to Q1 or to
any external Managing Steward. RMA holds its own veto and its own ratification. This
keeps RMA true to its founding principle — power stays distributed, no outside party
owns the hive.

> Open for the alliance: whether RMA later formalizes as a Q1 "hosted entity" is a
> future decision, not a precondition. We launch independent + aligned.

## Internal structure — the holarchy

- **The Alliance (Anchor Circle):** holds the unified mission and all domains.
- **Sub-Circles:** semi-autonomous groups (Media Production, Curriculum, Distribution,
  Governance) with explicit authority to self-organize.
- **Roles:** clear purposes + accountabilities, not static job descriptions.

Adapted from Q1's quorum pattern, and **this is RMA's answer to "no single person holds
excessive power"** (Ed):

- **Point** — a single member who coordinates and holds key decisions. A *leader, not
  an executive* — does not hold unilateral executive authority.
- **Triad** — three members who may hold executive authority, balancing the Point.
- **Term limits + elections** — defined in [`member-rights.md`](member-rights.md) and a
  forthcoming `roles-and-terms.md`. Roles rotate; no indefinite tenure.

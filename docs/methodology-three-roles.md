# Methodology — RMA · regenBEE · Smoking Tigers (as peers)

*A documented **model**, not a binding rule. From Josh's Campaign Framework. Reframed
per the alliance's discussion (Christine's objection, Ed's clarification) so that no
party "owns" another's domain.*

## The three roles — coordination, not ownership

Josh's framework names three collaborating bodies in the regenerative-media movement.
The original phrasing had each "owning" a domain; Christine rightly objected that
RMA being "just operations" under regenBEE's "ownership of strategy" *"doesn't feel
resonant."* Ed clarified the intent: *"less about 'own,' rather delegating and
coordinating... an internal committee or council."* So we frame them as **peers, each
holding a focus, none subordinate:**

| Body | Holds the focus of… | Relationship |
|------|--------------------|--------------|
| **RMA** | culture, activism, on-the-ground presence, the narrative commons | peer |
| **regenBEE** | strategic mapping + high-level vision (an advisory/council focus) | peer |
| **Smoking Tigers** | production pipeline + scaling content | peer |

Each is a self-governing body. They **coordinate** through shared projects and the
alliance's consent process — they do not command one another. Strategy is *offered*
(Josh's framework is a starting point to be reviewed and iterated by the willing and
skilled), not *imposed*.

## The campaign engine (Josh's framework, in brief)

- **Cadence — Hum · Sing · Shout:** constant low-lift presence (Hum) → consistent series
  like *Heat in the Streets* / *Soiled* (Sing) → major productions + the Manifesto (Shout).
- **Phasing — Spark · Fire · Sandbox · Renaissance:** relevancy → omnipresence →
  participation → self-sustaining flywheel.
- **MVP-first:** start with small pilots, evolve into anchor documentaries; align
  contributor incentives via Rev Points / future revenue share.
